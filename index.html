📊 How This Calculator Works

Early Claiming: Take Social Security now → withdraw less from your portfolio → more money stays invested.

Delayed Claiming: Wait for Social Security → withdraw more from your portfolio → less money stays invested.

The Goal: Find the strategy that leaves you with the most total wealth (in today's purchasing power) at your life expectancy.

Technical Methodology: This calculator performs a Nominal Cash Flow Analysis converted to Present Value (PV).

  • Step 1: We calculate future costs and benefits year-by-year using nominal percentages (expenses grow with inflation, investments grow with return rate).
  • Step 2: We determine if your portfolio can cover the gap between expenses and Social Security.
  • Step 3: We discount the final remaining portfolio value back to Today's Dollars so you can understand its real purchasing power.

🚨 Future Economic Uncertainties

The Urgency of Action: The window for implementing comprehensive Social Security reforms is narrowing as AI adoption accelerates. Unlike traditional economic downturns that allow for sector-based recovery, AI-driven unemployment presents unique challenges because displaced workers often need extensive retraining to shift into entirely new fields.

Without intervention, the Social Security system faces potential collapse from insufficient workers contributing to fund benefits - a scenario that could materialize much faster than the already-projected 2032 shortfall date.

🧮 Accuracy Note

Many simple calculators make the mistake of subtracting inflation from investment returns (Real Return) but then inflating expenses again, counting inflation twice. This calculator separates Investment Growth (Nominal) from Expense Inflation to provide accurate Actuarial outcomes.
